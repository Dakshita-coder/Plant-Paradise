Sunlight Basics

Sunlight is essential for photosynthesis, but every plant has different light needs. Too much can burn leaves; too little can cause dull, drooping growth.

  • Best Time: Morning sunlight (6‚Äì10 AM)
  • Type of Light: Direct, Indirect, or Partial Shade
  • Tip: Rotate your plants weekly for even growth.

Indoor Plants

Indoor plants usually thrive in indirect or filtered sunlight. Too much exposure can dry or burn delicate leaves.

  • Light Type: Bright, indirect light
  • Best Location: Near windows facing east or north
  • Examples: Money Plant, Snake Plant, Spider Plant
  • Tip: Use sheer curtains to filter harsh light.

Outdoor Plants

Outdoor plants love direct sunlight and open air. Some flowering and fruit-bearing varieties need at least 6 hours of sun daily.

  • Light Type: Full sunlight
  • Best Location: Open terrace, balcony, or garden
  • Examples: Rose, Hibiscus, Bougainvillea
  • Tip: Shift pots under shade during peak summer afternoons.

Low-Light Lovers

These plants can survive with minimal sunlight and are perfect for bedrooms or offices with limited natural light.

  • Light Type: Very low or indirect
  • Examples: ZZ Plant, Peace Lily, Pothos
  • Tip: Place them near LED grow lights if no window light is available.

Seasonal Sunlight Care

Light intensity changes with the seasons. Adjust placement based on weather and sunlight direction throughout the year.

  • Summer: Avoid afternoon heat; prefer morning sunlight.
  • Winter: Move plants closer to windows for maximum exposure.
  • Monsoon: Use artificial grow lights if days are too cloudy.
  • Tip: Dust leaves regularly ‚Äî clean leaves absorb light better!
