Watering Basics

Watering at the right time and quantity ensures strong roots and vibrant growth. Most plants prefer morning watering when sunlight can help dry excess moisture.

  • Best Time: Early morning or evening
  • Technique: Water near the soil base, not the leaves
  • Frequency: Depends on plant type & season
  • Tip: Always check topsoil moisture before watering

Indoor Plants

Indoor plants need less frequent watering due to less sunlight and lower evaporation. Overwatering is the most common mistake for indoor gardeners.

  • Frequency: Once every 3â€“4 days
  • Technique: Use a spray or gentle pour
  • Best Plants: Money Plant, Snake Plant, Peace Lily
  • Warning: Avoid waterlogging; ensure pot drainage holes

Outdoor Plants

Outdoor plants face direct sunlight and wind, increasing water loss. They generally require more frequent watering, especially during summer months.

  • Frequency: Daily during summer, alternate days otherwise
  • Technique: Use a watering can or drip irrigation
  • Best Plants: Rose, Hibiscus, Bougainvillea
  • Tip: Water early morning before strong sunlight
